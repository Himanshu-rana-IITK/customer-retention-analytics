# Customer Retention Analytics for a D2C Fashion Brand

## Overview

Analyzed transactional and behavioral data of 3,900 customers to identify loyalty drivers, discount dependency, and retention opportunities.

## Business Problem

The company relied heavily on promotional discounts but lacked visibility into customer loyalty and long-term retention.

## Methodology

Developed an Engagement-Based Loyalty (EBL) framework using:

- Previous Purchases (55%)
- Purchase Frequency Score (25%)
- Discount Absence Bonus (20%)

## Customer Segments

- Core Loyalists
- Deal Seekers
- Promo-Dependent Customers
- Low-Engagement Customers

## Key Findings

- 43% of revenue was discount-dependent
- Core Loyalists represented ~37% of customers
- Organic revenue accounted for ~57% of total revenue
- Purchase frequency emerged as the strongest predictor of loyalty

## Strategic Recommendations

- Reduce discount dependency below 30%
- Increase Core Loyalist share above 45%
- Implement a 12-month Promotional Sunset Plan
- Develop targeted retention campaigns for low-engagement customers
